T3 Radio

I studied Dieter Rams' 10 principles for good design and it forever changed the way I approach design. This T3 radio is a tribute to one of my most influential designers, Dieter Rams. Whether it be web, icon, motion or graphic design, I keep the principles in mind. For this project my goal was to build and render the most realistic looking T3 Radio.

Tools: Cinema 4D, Photoshop, Illustrator

“Good design is thorough down to the last detail. Nothing is left to chance. Care and accuracy in the design process show respect towards the user.” Dieter Rams

Here we see 2 images side by side. The one on the left is a render of the T3 Radio from a different camera. The image on the right is a wireframe view that lets us see the underlying 3d structure. This illustrates how the radio was constructed in 3d.

The lighting has an interesting interaction with the radio here. Looking at the corner of the device, we can see the sections of soft shadows that progressively darken as it nears the device.

It’s said that design is all in the details, so this is an intimate close up of the speaker grill, bevels and seams. I love the rim lights created by the lights naturally bouncing off each speaker grill.

I used the simple and beautiful software called Cinema 4D to model, texture, light and render the T3 Radio. This is a glimpse at my custom layout, scene setup and workflow.

When I began this project, I planned on making this a video project, but ultimately decided to keep it simple and focus just on the render. This is a concept frame for the video intro. The video begins with an empty light-gray frame and slowly a blurry T3 Radio emerges. The camera pans and dollys back slightly to reveal the outline of the radio. We notice that as the outline comes into view, the blurred T3 Radio also moves. We realize that the blurred radio is actually the cast shadow of the outlined radio. The camera pans over and the shadow leaves the screen. The BRAUN logo is revealed. We stay on this frame just long enough to read Braun. Then we would cut to rendered footage of the realistic looking T3 Radio.

After the video of the 3d T3 Radio, we conclude with a display of Dieter Rams' 10 principles for good design. And finally we crossfade to the final frame “Tribute to Dieter Rams”.
