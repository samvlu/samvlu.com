Bee Free

Google's philosophy for Android Honeycomb is free and open source. I was inspired by these principles and played with concepts for an art piece. I ran ideas by my friend Quang Tran and he inspired me to go with this one.

The bee is flying in an open field filled with honeycombs. It can fly anywhere it wishes. I think this captures the idea of free and open source because the bee is not restricted in anyway. It is free and in an open field.

A helio airship, artillery, scout, and tank.

Tools: Zebra Jimnie Gel Rollerball

“Open source is a beautiful
way of collaborating”
Douglas Rushkoff

I began this project by focusing on the bee— I modeled all the parts, textured it’s features and set up the lighting. Next I built a simple hilly environment with a blue atmosphere.

I decided the environment needed some elements. Honeycombs! The next step was to create honeycombs of different sizes. To experiment even further, I created materials and set up some nice lighting. The materials proved to be the most challenging. I wanted the honeycomb to look vibrant and full of life but have depth. I was happy after creating a custom subsurface scattering material.

Finally I had the idea of filling in some of the honeycomb with honey so I tested methods of achieving a honey-like look. The technique I liked the most was building the honey with geometry and using the right materials to match the look of honey. This is one of my renders along with a wireframe view.
