Warships That Walk

Over 4 days, I spent about 25 mintutes sketching warships with legs that walk like animals or insects. While sketching I tried to imagine how each vehicle would actually move—what kind of energy powered each one. And finally I always framed the image with a sense of scale—to help visualize the size of each warship.

A helio airship, artillery, scout, and tank.

Tools: Zebra Jimnie Gel Rollerball

This interesting machine is called the Airship. It got it’s name because it’s equipped with propellers that allow it to fly. Not only can it hover over it’s prey, but it’s got extra long legs for lifting anything into the air. The Airships’ dome shaped windows give the pilot many vantage points. This Airship can also produce Mini-Airships that follow it’s command and can relay information.

I call this the Scout because it’s made to move really fast and is not designed for combat. It’s main weapon for defense are the sharp kneedle-like protrusions at it’s knees.

This warship is the Artillery. It has strong legs that protects it’s core but results in slow mobility. The core has a 360º window for seeing targets from every direction and directly below the core are long range weapons. And finally this vehicle is equipped with a solar panel for absorbing the sun’s energy. It also has a long distance satellite communicator for receiving messages from the team closer to the combat.

This is the Tank. It’s a close quarters combat vehicle that is quick on the field and equipped with a mid-ranged weapon that can rotate 360º. The tank is designed to be right in heart of the battlefield.
