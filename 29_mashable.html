Mashable Flying Toasters

At Mashable 2011, Idea Integration had the privilege of setting up a booth at the event. One of the activities was a game inspired by After Dark's "Flying Toasters Screensaver".

I worked on the in-game 3d graphics by building a joystick, flying toaster and spatula. After completing the game graphics, I created 2 posters to promote the event.

Tools: Cinema 4D, Photoshop, Illustrator

I began designing the toaster and joystick by sketching orthographic views and interesting angles. I used Illustrator to build different curves to use in modeling the toaster. I used Cinema 4D to model, texture, light and render all the bread, toaster, wings and joystick elements. And the rest of the poster elements were created using Photoshop.

Here we see the wireframes of the joystick and the toaster. The joystick consists of many simple shapes like a rounded rectangle, cylinders and a sphere. The Toaster is a bit more complex. We have mechanical elements like the toaster and organic items like the wings and bread. The joystick and toaster are constructed with separate 3d pieces.

Before diving into 3d, I took time to sketch orthographic views to make sure I understood how the toaster looked at each angle. These sketches served as a great guide when I started building it in 3d.
