Aircrafts

I really like aircrafts so I spent a few days sketching different types. I used a pen to practice drawing lines with intention. The focus for this sketch was perspective, basic form, and realism. I spent 30 minutes on each sketch.

A jet, airliner, warship, and aircar.

Tools: Blue Bic Round Pen, Pilot G2 Gel Roller, Zebra Jimnie Gel Rollerball

I sketched this jet during one of my quick breaks from the mouse and keyboard. To begin, I found a good reference image online. Then, with a blue bic pen, I lightly sketched the perspective lines. I proceeded to mark the proportions of the jet with dots throughout the canvas. Once I got a good sense of where each element should be, I just started sketching while use the dots and perspective lines as my guides.

While waiting at the airport, I looked out the window and for some reason started wondering how planes actually work. Because I was curious, I started noticing all the details I usually overlook, like the screws on the wings. I took out my sketch book and started sketching.
